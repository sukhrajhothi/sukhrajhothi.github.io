Prototyping

Machining, 3D Printing, Casting ... you name it!

I Love Prototyping!/h4>

My first protoyping skill was learning to 3D print. I remember I had my first 3D printer in grade 10 and I was able to get my first taste of reapid prototyping. I obviosuly did learn that 3D printing was not the one step solution. A lot has to be done to get a nice finsihed product. I've learned to model parts to be printed in SLS, FDM, and SLA printer, furthermore I have persoanlly leanred how to print in FDM and SLA formats. I just recently acqurimed my own Formlabs FORM 2 priner which has been a dream to work on. I've worked on projects where we have large SLS prints that need to be brought to a finished professional presetnation look. These projects have taught me the pros and cons of 3D printing. I also am skilled at machining parts, there are obvious times where 3D printing is not the solution. A better solution maybe CNC milling or using a lathe to get the desired shape and fit.
